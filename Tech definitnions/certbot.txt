Certbot is a **free, open-source tool** developed by the Electronic Frontier 
Foundation (EFF) that helps you automatically get and renew SSL/TLS certificates
 from [Let’s Encrypt](https://letsencrypt.org/).

In simple terms:

* 🔒 **Purpose** → It enables HTTPS on your website (so browsers show the padlock instead of “Not Secure”).
* ⚡ **Automation** → Certbot can automatically fetch, install, and renew your certificates before they expire (Let’s Encrypt certs are valid for 90 days).
* 🌍 **Compatibility** → Works with most popular web servers (like **Nginx**, **Apache**) and operating systems (Ubuntu, Debian, CentOS, etc.).
* 🆓 **Cost** → 100% free (Let’s Encrypt provides the certificates at no charge).

---

### Typical workflow with Certbot

1. Install Certbot (`apt install certbot python3-certbot-nginx` for Nginx, or `python3-certbot-apache` for Apache).
2. Run something like:

   ```bash
   sudo certbot --nginx -d yourdomain.com -d www.yourdomain.com
   ```

   Certbot talks to Let’s Encrypt, verifies your domain ownership, gets the cert, and updates your Nginx config automatically.
3. Certificates auto-renew in the background via a systemd timer or cron job.
